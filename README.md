The Pulsar and Gravitational Physics Group of the National Astronomical Observatories, Chinese Academy of Sciences

Our research group, established in 2020 at the National Astronomical Observatories of China (NAOC), focuses on pulsar searches and analysis, testing gravity theories, galaxy formation and evolution, radio astronomy technology, digital techniques, and the application of artificial intelligence in astronomy. We have been actively involved in the construction and upgrading of major Chinese radio astronomy facilities, including FAST and 21CMA. In addition, our team members play key roles in major research projects related to FAST pulsars and fast radio bursts (FRBs), funded by the Ministry of Science and Technology of China and the National Natural Science Foundation of China. We are also actively engaged in pre-research for the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) in China.
